Will programmers be replaced with robots in the near-future?

No, but probably not the reason most people would think. Robots can't do anything but insert tab a into slot b type jobs. While much of what is currently considered programming could be automated and a lot of the research I do could also be speed up by something like a good search tool, still a significant part of my work has been either about doing something no one has ever really done, trying to figure out how to do a task in a more efficient and effective manner or mapping requirements (often provided in an ambiguous way by people who understand their business but not information systems) to information systems development.

Robotics implies movement. It would not take movement or motion apparatus of any kind to replace a programmer. Rather, code could be created entirely in silico without even a keyboard. That being said, I doubt AI itself will replace programmers at any point in the near future. Instead, I think AI will cause developers to be forced to become much more productive by leveraging AI itself.
